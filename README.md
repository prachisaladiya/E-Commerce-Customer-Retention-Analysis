# E-Commerce Customer Retention Analysis

## Problem Statement
An E-commerce company observed that its revenue was stable, but growth had slowed down. At the same time, repeat purchases were declining.  
The objective was to identify:
- Where the problem exists  
- Which customers are affected  
- What actions can improve retention and growth  

---

## Objective
To analyze customer and transaction data and identify key issues in customer retention and revenue growth.

---

## Tools Used
- Python (Pandas) – Data cleaning and analysis  
- Power BI – Dashboard creation and visualization  

---

## Approach
- Cleaned and analyzed the dataset using Pandas  
- Studied monthly revenue trends to understand growth  
- Performed cohort analysis to track customer retention  
- Analyzed order value distribution to identify high-value orders  
- Identified top customers and their revenue contribution  
- Built a Power BI dashboard to present insights  

---

## Key Insights
- Total revenue was around **₹17M**, but growth was not consistent  
- **Top 20% customers contributed ~80% of total revenue**  
- Out of 890 orders:  
  - **331 orders were above ₹20K**  
  - **241 orders were above ₹30K**  
- Customer retention **drops significantly after the first purchase**  

---

## What It Means
- Business is highly dependent on a small group of high-value customers  
- Low repeat purchase rate is affecting growth  
- Revenue is stable but not increasing due to poor retention  

---

## Recommendations
- Focus on retaining high-value customers through loyalty programs  
- Improve repeat purchases using email marketing and remarketing  
- Increase average order value using upselling and bundle offers  

---

## Dashboard
The Power BI dashboard includes:
- Revenue trend over time  
- Customer retention (cohort analysis)  
- High-value order analysis  
- Top customer contribution  

---

## Conclusion
The analysis highlights that improving customer retention and focusing on high-value customers can help the business achieve better long-term growth.
